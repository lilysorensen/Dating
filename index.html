    • Dating Terminology and Definitions

    Date - (noun) an activity lasting roughly 2-5 hours in which two people participate who may or may not like each other.

    Date/Dating - (verb) you are “dating” someone if you are: a) currently on a date with that person, or b) have gone on two or more dates with a particular person and there is a likelihood you will go on another date with said person in the near future.

    Relationship - (noun) We all have simple or complex relationships with the people in our lives. In the realm of dating, we are usually referring to romantic relationships or exclusive* relationships. Usually, both parties tacitly agree to enter a relationship.

    Break-up - (noun/verb) - A break-up has occurred when both people in a relationship know that they are no longer exclusively dating. Both parties don’t need to agree to the break-up; in fact, a break-up is usually executed by one person. There are lots of legitimate reasons to break up with someone: you don’t like the other person anymore, you like someone else, you want to date more/different people.

    Serious - (adj.) The “seriousness” of a relationship is directly proportional to how devastated one or both people in a relationship would be if a break up were to occur. One might suddenly find themselves in a “Serious” relationship, only because they discovered their dating partner has much stronger feelings than they initially realized. (e.g. Whoa! We were more serious than I originally thought.)

    Exclusive - (adj.) Dating someone exclusively means that you deliberately eschew dating other people. Some might make their relationship exclusive because they don’t want to date other people. Others want to test the waters of being committed to exactly one person (probably to see if they can tolerate/enjoy spending lots of time with just one person.) Sometimes, people find themselves in an exclusive relationship with someone, but they’re not going out on any dates. This is lame.
